Conference Venue

C.A.R.L. Auditorium

The venue for the in-person part of the conference will be the C.A.R.L. auditorium (Central Auditorium for Research and Learning) in Aachen. The name of course alludes to Charlemagne, the famous emperor whose imperial capital city was Aachen.

C.A.R.L. Hörsaalgebäude
Claßenstraße 14, 52062 Aachen
Rooms: t.b.a.

Getting there

    By Bus

The C.A.R.L. auditorium is located in close proximity to selveral bus stations:

  • Audimax (buses 1, 3A, 3B, 16, 30, 33, 41, 73, 74, 173), 1-minute-walk to C.A.R.L. auditorium
  • Westbahnhof (buses 1, 3A, 3B, 16, 30, 33, 41, 73, 74, 173), 5-minute-walk to C.A.R.L. auditorium
  • Ponttor (buses 1, 3A, 3B, 13A, 13B, 16, 30, 33, 41, 73, 74, 173), 5-minute-walk to C.A.R.L. auditorium
You can look up any connections via the websites of Aachen's public transport company ASEAG ( www.aseag.de ). Unfortunately, this website is only available in German. For English travel information, you may want to switch to other offers such as Google Maps.

    By Train

From the train station "Aachen West", it will take you only about a 5-minutes-walk to get to C.A.R.L. Several local traffic trains (RE4, RE18, RB20, RB33) arive there regularly. If you arrive in Aachen at a different train station ("Aachen Hauptbahnhof", which ist the main station, "Aachen Rote Erde" or "Aachen Schanz", you can either change trains, take the bus, or walk.

    By Car

Please note that the venue is located in an Environmental Zone ("Umweltzone").

Parking is sometimes hard to find in Aachen. As everything tends to be close by, we advise you to come to the conference venue by public transport or by foot.
